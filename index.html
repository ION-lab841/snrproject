Welcome to the Math HL 1 Midterm Study Guide

Topic 1: Functions

Overview: This topic introduces the concept of a function as a relationship between variables, using set notations and graphing. The topic contains domain and range, function notation, and types of functions such as linear, quadratic, exponential, and piecewise. Throughout this exploration students will gain the ability to transform functions through translations, reflections, and stretches.

Notes for Topic 1: Functions

Topic 2: Quadratic and Absolute Value Functions

Overview: In this unit it covers quadratic and absolute value functions, focusing on solving inequalities by first converting them into equations and using test points to determine valid intervals. For quadratic inequalities, the discriminant helps identify the number of solutions, while the sum and product of roots provide more information about the equations' behavior. Absolute value expression and solved by breaking up the absolute value expression into its positive and negative cases. With transformations like g(x)=∣f(x)∣ reflecting graph parts above the x-axis. The notes also highlight how squaring or applying absolute values to functions alters their graphs, affecting y-values and symmetry.

Notes for Topic 2: Quadratic and Absolute Value

Topic 3: Polynomial and Rational Functions

Overview: This topic introduces various methods of finding the roots/zeros of a rational function of any exponential power. This topic features synthetic division, remainder theorem, product and sum, etc. Throughout this exploration, students will gain the ability to simplify rational functions to any power to its zeroes as well as find where the intersection of the x-axis are located.

Notes for Topic 3: Polynomial and Rational Functions

Topic 4: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Overview: This topic introduces the new functions in the form of exponential and logarithmic. This topic features converting exponential functions to logarithmic function and vice versa. Additionally students are introduced to exponential and logarithmic laws for solving and rewriting these functions. Throughout the exploration, students will gain the ability to solve and graph logarithmic and exponential functions.

Notes for Topic 4: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Topic 5: Sequences, Series, and Methods of Proof

Overview: This topic introduces arithmetic and geometric sequences and series as well as proof by induction and contradiction. This topic features finding the common difference or ratio between each number in the series and finding any specific term in the series as well as the sum of the series. Throughout the exploration, students will gain the ability to work with arithmetic and geometric series as well as prove mathematical statements with contradiction and induction.

Notes for Topic 5: Sequences, Series, and Methods of Proof

Topic 6: Trig Intro and Modelling(Trig appetizer)

Overview: This topic introduces the concept of trigonometry. Students are introduced to using sine, cosine, and tangent to find a triangle's side length as well as graphing different trig functions. Students will learn and understand different parts of a trig function affects the graph and transformations.

Notes for Topic 6: Trig Intro and Modelling(Trig appetizer)

Topic 7: Trig Identities, Inverses and Equations(Trig Entree)

Overview: This topic expands on the concept of trignometry by introducing more trignometry identities and inverse trig graphs. Students will use trignometry identities to solve for angles and side lengths through different methods such as substitution.

Notes for Topic 7: Trig Identities, Inverses and Equations(Trig Entree)

Topic 8: Trig and Triangles(Trig Dessert)

Overview: This topic of trignometry is primarily focused on using trignometry identities to solve for angles and sides in triangles. Students will learn solve for missing sides or missing angles with what they're already given.
